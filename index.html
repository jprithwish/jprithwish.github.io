I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science at Georgia Tech, USA, advised by Prof. Vijay Ganesh and Prof. Sriram Vishwanath. I spent one year of my Ph.D. at the University of Waterloo, Canada.

I build AI systems that can reason, verify, and self-correct: grounding the empirical power of large language models (LLMs) in symbolic and formal frameworks, with the long-term goal of systems that are not just capable, but can certify their own correctness.

My research lies at the intersection of automated reasoning and machine learning, developing neuro-symbolic techniques that enhance LLMs at both the training and inference stage. I specialize in combining symbolic reasoning tools and formal methods with LLMs through reinforcement learning, semantic alignment, and other techniques to significantly advance their capabilities in software engineering (code translation, generation, and repair) and mathematical reasoning (proof auto-formalization, synthesis, and discovery in Lean). My work has appeared at ICSE, ICLR, IJCAI, ECAI, WWW, and received a Distinguished Paper Award at ICSE 2026.

Research Interests Neuro-Symbolic AI Formal Methods AI for Software Engineering AI for Mathematics Programming Languages & Compilers Large Language Models (LLMs)

Previously, I earned my Master's degree in Computer Science from IIT Kharagpur (Institute Silver Medalist, 2022) and my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Jadavpur University (University Gold Medalist, 2020), graduating at the top of my class at both the institutes. In Summer 2025, I interned at Amazon Science (AWS, Seattle) as an Applied Scientist with the Next Gen DevX team, designing LLM-based coding agents. I am also a recipient of the Reliance Foundation Scholarship for AI & CS (2021), and prestigious national awards JBNSTS, KVPY, and NTSE from the Government of India. Here is my CV.
